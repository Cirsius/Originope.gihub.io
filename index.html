      Chaos;Gate

        A short story featuring an appearance from Steins;Gate's protagonist Rintaro Okabe. Takes place during Chapter 7.

      After finishing the true ending:

      Delusion of Zero

        A short story monologue set 18 months before the events of Chaos;Head NoAH.

      Chaos;Head Love Chu Chu! (untranslated)

        A fandisk visual novel that acts as a direct sequel to Chaos;Head NoAH. Unlikely to receive an official English localization.
        Chaos;Child — particularly its true ending — is better informed by some knowledge that Chaos;Head Love Chu Chu! provides, but it is otherwise safely skippable if you cannot read Japanese.


    Buy on Steam | Download improvement patch | Guide


      Steins;Gate Elite (improvement patch coming soon)

        An updated re-release of Steins;Gate that uses footage from the anime adaptation instead of traditional character sprites and illustrations.
        Only recommended for replays. First-time readers are strongly advised to choose the original version over Elite.

      After finishing the true ending:

      Holy Day of the Calamitous Birth

        A novella that acts as a direct sequel to Steins;Gate.

      Egoistic Poriomania

        A bonus OVA episode that takes place after Steins;Gate's events in the anime adaptation's own separate canon.

      Load Region of Déjà Vu

        A movie that takes place after Egoistic Poriomania in the anime adaptation's own separate canon.

      A Posteriori Existence

        A drama CD that acts as a prequel to Load Region of Déjà Vu.

      Steins;Gate: Variant Space Octet

        A retro text adventure game set after the events of the visual novel, with some connections to Chaos;Head.
        Some elements first introduced here reappear with more importance later on in the Science Adventure series.

      Steins;Gate: My Darling's Embrace (improvement patch coming soon | Guide)

        A romantic comedy-themed spinoff fandisk set on a peaceful worldline in the Delta attractor field.

      Steins;Gate: Linear Bounded Phenogram (improvement patch coming soon)

        A spinoff fandisk consisting of eleven short stories written by various guest writers, most of which focus on different characters from Steins;Gate's cast.
        This is best saved for after Robotics;Notes Elite, as two of the stories contain references that can be considered spoilers.

      Babel of the Grieved Maze

        A drama CD with manga and light novel adaptations that follows Kurisu during the events of Chapter 10: Paradox Meltdown.

      Hyde of the Dark Dimension

        A drama CD that takes place on a Gamma attractor field worldline, repurposed from what was originally a Moeka side route that was cut from the visual novel.

      The Distant Valhalla

        A short story that takes place in the Alpha attractor field, detailing the origins of Okabe's resistance group Valkyrie.

      Braunian Motion of Love and Hate

        A prequel manga that explores Yuugo "Mr. Braun" Tennouji's backstory, his early encounters with his building's original landlord and how he came to own it.

      Amity of Budding Reminiscence (untranslated)

        A short story detailing how Okabe and Daru first met and became friends.

      Winter Sky Rocket (untranslated)

        Listen immediately after finishing Phase 7: Ionosphere Driver if you can understand Japanese.

      The Unpublished Memoirs of Senomiya Misaki

        A prequel light novel with a manga adaptation that explores Misaki Senomiya's backstory. Read as soon as you reach Phase 12: Heliosphere Connector.

      After finishing the true ending:

      Dream Seeker (untranslated)

      Maps, the Rain, and Macarons

        A short story focusing on Toshiyuki Sawada that takes place during Phase 9: Exosphere Breaker.

      Tomorrow in the Box

        An interlinked crossover story featuring the protagonists of all 3 previous mainline games. Can be read at any time once Chaos;Head NoAH,
        Steins;Gate         and Robotics;Notes Elite have all been finished.

      After finishing the true ending:

      The Memoirs of a Certain Wrong-Sider (untranslated)

        A light novel retelling of Chaos;Child's events from multiple different perspectives, primarily, Nono Kurusu's.

      Slumbering Fools

        A drama CD that explores Mio Kunosato's backstory.

      Children's Collapse (partially translated, ongoing)

        A prequel manga series that further explores Mio Kunosato's backstory, as well as her relationship with Steins;Gate's Kurisu Makise.

      Children's Collapse: First Time Going Out

        A short story set between chapters 3 and 4 of Children's Collapse.

      Chaos;Child Love Chu Chu!! (untranslated)

        A fandisk visual novel that acts as a direct sequel to Chaos;Child. Highly unlikely to ever receive an official English localization due to NSFW content.
        Robotics;Notes DaSH mildly benefits from some knowledge that the true ending provides, but it is otherwise safely skippable if you cannot read Japanese.

      Children's Revive (poor translation)

        A light novel set some time after the visual novel that acts as an epilogue for the Chaos;Child portion of the Science Adventure series.
        The only English translation currently available is readable, but of poor quality.


    Buy on Steam | Download improvement patch | Guide

      After finishing the true ending:

      23β: Divide by Zero

        An alternate version of Episode 23 of the Steins;Gate anime adaptation, featuring a different ending that leads into Steins;Gate 0.
        This should be started immediately after finishing the Milky Way Crossing ending in the visual novel, followed by the below anime adaptation.

      Steins;Gate 0 anime adaptation

        The Steins;Gate 0 anime is not a straightforward adaptation of the visual novel, but rather an extension of it that shows the events that took place
        on the Milky Way Crossing ending's worldline that ultimately led to the execution of Operation Skuld.

      Paradise Lost in Adaptive Radiation

        An unfinished drama CD, made of repurposed material from a Fubuki-centric route that was cut from the visual novel.

    Watch on Crunchyroll or Funimation

    Occultic;Nine is in an unusual situation; its canonical connection to the rest of the Science Adventure series isn't completely clear and the original light novel series
    has been left incomplete. Reading the light novels and then watching the anime adaptation immediately afterward is currently considered to be the best way to experience Occultic;Nine.
